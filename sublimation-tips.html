Quick Answer: Standard Sublimation Settings

For most sublimation blanks, use 385-400°F (195-205°C) temperature with medium to firm pressure. Mugs press at 400°F for 4-5 minutes, t-shirts at 385-400°F for 45-60 seconds. Always print at 300 DPI with sublimation ink on sublimation paper.

Critical: Use 65%+ polyester content for vibrant transfers. Cotton or low-poly blends will produce faded results.

Sublimation Temperature & Time Chart

These settings are starting points. Always test with your specific equipment and blanks, as heat presses vary in accuracy by 10-15°F.

Important: These are general guidelines. Your heat press may run hotter or colder than displayed. Invest in an infrared thermometer to verify actual platen temperature, and always do test presses before production runs.

Mug Sublimation Guide

Mugs are one of the most popular sublimation items. Follow these steps for consistent, professional results.

Design Specifications

  • Resolution: 300 DPI
  • 11oz Mug Size: 2200 × 900 px
  • 15oz Mug Size: 2600 × 1100 px
  • Color Mode: RGB
  • Mirror Image: Yes (Flip Horizontal)

Press Settings

  • Temperature: 400°F (204°C)
  • Time (11oz): 4-5 min
  • Time (15oz): 5-6 min
  • Pressure: Medium-Firm

Step-by-Step Process

1

Print design mirrored on sublimation paper

2

Clean mug with lint roller and alcohol wipe

3

Position design and tape with heat tape

4

Preheat mug press to 400°F

5

Press for 4-5 minutes with firm pressure

6

Remove paper while still warm (not hot)

T-Shirt Press Settings

Design Size (300 DPI)

  • Small Logo: 2400 × 2400 px
  • Chest Print: 3600 × 3600 px
  • Full Front: 4200 × 5400 px

Press Settings

  • Temperature: 385-400°F
  • Time: 45-60 sec
  • Pressure: Medium

Pro Tip: Always pre-press polyester shirts for 5 seconds to remove moisture and wrinkles. This prevents "gassing out" which causes white spots or blurry edges on your transfer.

Tumbler Sublimation Guide

Tumblers require special handling due to their curved surface. You can use either a convection oven with shrink wrap or a tumbler press.

Convection Oven Method

Recommended for full wraps

  • Temperature: 365-375°F
  • Time: 6-7 minutes
  • Use shrink wrap sleeve
  • Rotate halfway through

Tumbler Press Method

For partial designs

  • Temperature: 385-400°F
  • Time: 50-60 sec per section
  • Rotate and repeat for full wrap
  • Watch for seam lines

Warning: Never use a kitchen oven for sublimation. Sublimation ink releases gases during the process that can contaminate food. Use a dedicated convection oven or tumbler press only.

Common Sublimation Mistakes

Avoid these frequent errors that ruin sublimation projects and waste expensive blanks.

1

Ghosting / Double Image

Caused by paper shifting during pressing or removing paper while too hot.

Fix: Use more heat tape, ensure firm pressure, and let cool slightly before removing paper.

2

Faded or Dull Colors

Usually caused by low polyester content, insufficient temperature, or expired ink.

Fix: Use 65%+ polyester, verify press temperature, and check ink expiration date.

3

White Spots / Gassing Out

Moisture escaping from the blank causes white blotches on the design.

Fix: Pre-press blanks for 5-10 seconds to release moisture before applying design.

4

Forgetting to Mirror

Design appears backwards on the finished product.

Fix: Always flip horizontal before printing. Make this part of your pre-print checklist.

5

Uneven Pressure

Parts of the design transfer well while other areas are faded or blank.

Fix: Check that your heat press platens are parallel. Use pressure paper to test distribution.

6

Scorch Marks

Brown or yellow discoloration on the substrate around the design.

Fix: Lower temperature or reduce press time. Some fabrics are heat-sensitive.

Pro Tips for Better Sublimation

Use Fresh Ink

Sublimation ink expires. Use within 6 months of opening for best color vibrancy.

Print Same Day

Don't let printed designs sit for days. The ink can dry out and transfer poorly.

Lint Roll Everything

Even invisible dust creates white spots. Lint roll every blank before pressing.

Use Butcher Paper

Protect your press with butcher paper on top. Replace after every few presses.

Test New Blanks

Every blank brand is different. Test time and temp before production runs.

Vent Your Space

Sublimation releases gases. Work in a well-ventilated area or use a fume extractor.

Heat Tape Matters

Cheap tape melts or leaves residue. Invest in quality heat-resistant tape.

Keep a Log

Record time, temp, pressure for each blank type. Perfect your settings over time.
