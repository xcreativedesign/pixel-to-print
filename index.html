How It Works

Understanding DPI and print dimensions is essential for quality output. Here's the science behind it.

1

Enter Your Dimensions

Input your image's pixel width, height, and the intended DPI (dots per inch) for printing. 300 DPI is standard for high-quality prints.

2

Instant Calculation

Our tool instantly converts pixels to physical measurements using the formula: Inches = Pixels ÷ DPI. Results are shown in inches, centimeters, and millimeters.

3

Quality Check

The smart quality indicator warns you if your image resolution is too low for the intended print size, helping you avoid pixelated or blurry prints.

Understanding DPI

DPI (Dots Per Inch) determines print quality. Higher DPI means more detail and sharper prints:

300+ DPI

Excellent for photos, magazines, brochures

150-300 DPI

Good for posters, banners, large prints

72-150 DPI

Acceptable for very large signs, billboards

72 DPI

Standard for web/screen viewing only
